Mission

  • Release well-crafted open-source applications that demonstrate the unique potential of Stellar.
  • Provide a high-level open-source development kit to build Stellar applications.
  • Implement and promote a way to secure users funds by decoupling wallets from other services (CosmicLink protocol, SEP-0007).
  • Defend the interests of the community by promoting balanced & vendor-neutral solutions at the protocol level (CAP, SEP).
  • Promote free software as a solution to globally raise the quality of the Stellar ecosystem.

Philosophy

Cosmic.Plus embraces the cooperative mindset of the free software community. Each Cosmic.Plus product gets released in the public domain, under the MIT license.

In a competitive space such as the Stellar ecosystem, most actors strive to create an edge against competitors. The drawback is that features that could benefit the whole space are generally jailed in private code repositories.

By releasing software & libraries in the public domain, Cosmic.Plus globally improve Stellar's network quality. Instead of coding the same functionalities, again and again, developers are offered an opportunity to focus on what makes their software unique.

Economic Model

It is always a bit tricky to profitably develop open-source software over the long-term. So far, Cosmic.Plus financed itself thanks to programs such as the Stellar Build Contest or the Stellar Community Fund

While the research & development budget is not secured yet, a fund exclusively dedicated to long-term maintenance has been put aside. This guarantees that, in any scenario, Cosmic.Plus existing products will remain up-to-date for years.

There are ongoing plans to make Cosmic.Plus financially independent without sacrificing its ethic. Realistically, this could take a couple of years to achieve.

Privacy

Privacy is a right. Cosmic.Plus applications and libraries don't use ads nor trackers and don't collect or publish data about users activity. However, Cosmic.Plus web applications are distributed through GitHub, Cloudflare and to a lesser extent Azure. Those services may keep logs about user activity.
